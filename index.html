Background

The Goal of the Waikīkī Transportation Management Association is to move people and goods in and out of Waikīkī BETTER.

We are a formal organization, established through public-private-partnership, through the Honolulu City Council.

Current priorities include traffic management, freight loading/unloading improvements, and pedestrian safety.

Quick facts
  • Founded: 2017
  • Service area: Waikīkī
  • Focus: Traffic, freight deliveries, safety
About

History

The Waikīkī Transportation Management Association (WTMA) was established in 2015 by Honolulu City Council ordinance with support from Waikīkī stakeholders in the public and private sectors. Founding partners included the Waikīkī Improvement Association, major tour and transportation companies, and other community representatives. The Association was created to respond to growing demands on Waikīkī’s transportation network as visitor, resident, and employee numbers increased. WTMA’s first initiative, launched in 2016, focused on improving passenger loading along Royal Hawaiian Avenue. Since then, WTMA has continued working to coordinate curb use, parking, and mobility strategies that improve access in and around Waikīkī.
