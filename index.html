Background

The Goal of the Waikīkī Transportation Management Association is to move people and goods in and out of Waikīkī BETTER.

We are a formal organization, established through public-private-partnership, through the Honolulu City Council. Current priorities include traffic management, freight loading/unloading improvements, and pedestrian safety.

About

History

The Waikīkī Transportation Management Association (WTMA) is a nonprofit corporation formally organized in 2018 at the request of the Waikīkī Improvement Association (WIA). It was established through legislation enacted in September 2017, creating the Waikīkī Transportation Management Special Improvement District No. 4.

WTMA was born in response to a growing problem: curb space abuse. The WTMA operates under a Memorandum of Agreement with the City and County of Honolulu's Department of Transportation Services (DTS) to manage the public right of way, parking facilities, and related infrastructure within Waikīkī in an effective, equitable, sustainable, and safe manner.

Since its inception, WTMA has worked with stakeholders to improve traffic flow, optimize curb usage for loading and unloading, and enhance pedestrian safety. Early initiatives included revising traffic management on Royal Hawaiian Avenue, installing regulatory signs and sensors, creating new loading zone management systems with permits and reservations, extensive communications programs with city departments and vehicle operators, targeted enforcement efforts, and pilot loading zone demonstrations with advanced vehicle detection technologies.

Key milestones
